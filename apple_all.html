Agridera Fresh Market
Apple Hybrids

*****

Agridera aims to be one of the world's leading companies in the breeding, production, and marketing of apple seeds. We invest intensively in research and development to provide solutions to growers and consumers in numerous countries around the world. In particular, we breed improved disease-resistant cultivars adapted to different markets globally.

Currently, emphasis is placed on developing varieties resistant to apple scab, powdery mildew, and other fungal diseases. Additionally, efforts are being made to create varieties that are resistant to low temperatures and drought conditions.

In terms of horticultural traits, breeding is focused on earliness, various fruit sizes, firm texture, color, aroma, resistance to mechanical damage, shelf life, and tolerance to temperature fluctuations. Combinations of high-quality features and disease resistance are sought in a variety of apple types, including round and elongated shapes.

Agridera's apple breeding programs focus on three main segments: Greenhouse Apples, Open-field Apples, and Dwarf Varieties for Orchards.

Greenhouse Apple Breeding Program

This breeding is aimed for the European and North American markets. Focused on producing early-ripening apples with round fruit shape and resistances to apple scab, powdery mildew, and root diseases. Some hybrids may also include additional resistances such as fire blight and bacterial canker.

Open-field
Apples

This breeding is for the Australian, New Zealand, and South American markets. The main focus is on large, crisp apples (200 grams plus), with resistances to apple scab, powdery mildew, and drought tolerance. In the near future, we will also incorporate resistance to apple rust and other regional diseases into our cultivars.

Dwarf Varieties for
Orchards

This breeding is aimed for small-scale orchards and intensive farming systems. We breed compact, productive apple trees with high-quality round and elongated fruit shapes, ranging from 150 to 250 grams. These hybrids combine resistance to apple scab, powdery mildew, and fire blight as basic resistances, with some hybrids potentially including further resistances, such as root rot and drought tolerance.

Agridera operates through international and local distributers around the world.
